title: "Tracking and Resolving Student Absence Follow-Up Records"
description: "Document and close out reasons for student absences and late arrivals in Elimu Bora, upload evidence files, and keep a complete audit trail."
---

A follow-up is a formal record that captures why a student was absent or arrived late. Rather than leaving an absence unacknowledged, follow-ups give teachers and administrators a structured way to collect explanations, gather supporting evidence, and close the loop — creating a complete picture of attendance for every student.

## How follow-ups work

Every attendance record with a status of **Absent** or **Late** can have a follow-up attached to it. When you mark a student absent or late, the system automatically creates a follow-up in **Pending** status. That follow-up stays open until a teacher or administrator reviews it and marks it resolved.

The follow-up lifecycle is straightforward:

```
Pending → Resolved
```

When a follow-up is assigned to a staff member, they receive a notification — both in-app and by email — so nothing falls through the cracks. Every change made to a follow-up is recorded in the activity audit trail, giving you a full history of who did what and when.

## Creating a follow-up

Follow-ups for **Absent** and **Late** records are created automatically when you mark an attendee with those statuses. You don't need to create them manually in most cases.

If you want to add details to a newly created follow-up straight away, you can do so from the register view:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the register">
    Navigate to **Registers** and click on the register that contains the absence or late record you want to follow up on.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Go to the Follow-Ups tab">
    Inside the register, click the **Follow-Ups** tab. You'll see a list of all follow-ups for that register. By default, the list is filtered to show **Pending** follow-ups only.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Find the follow-up record">
    Locate the follow-up for the student you want to document. The list shows the attendee's name, their attendance status (Absent or Late), and the expected vs actual check-in and check-out times for context.
  </Step>

  <Step title="View or edit the follow-up">
    Click the **View** icon to see the full details of the follow-up. From the detail view, you can see the attendee information, attendance times, resolution status, and any supporting document that has been attached.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Resolving a follow-up

Once the reason for an absence has been established — for example, the parent called in, or a medical note was received — you can mark the follow-up as resolved.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Locate the pending follow-up">
    Go to **Registers**, open the relevant register, and click the **Follow-Ups** tab. Alternatively, navigate directly to **Registers > Follow-Ups** and filter by **Pending** status to see all open follow-ups across all registers.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Resolve">
    In the follow-up row, click the **Resolve** action button. A modal opens with two fields:

    - **Resolution Notes** — enter the reason for the absence or late arrival (e.g., "Parent called to advise student had a medical appointment" or "Medical note received from St. Francis Hospital").
    - **Supporting Document (Optional)** — upload any evidence file, such as a medical note, a permission letter, or a parent communication.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit the resolution">
    Click **Resolve** to confirm. The follow-up status changes to **Resolved**, and the system records your name as the person who resolved it. The resolution is also logged in the activity audit trail.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Uploading a supporting document — such as a scanned medical note or a photo of a signed permission letter — creates a permanent, verifiable record attached directly to the follow-up. PDFs and images (JPG, PNG) are both accepted, up to 5 MB per file.
</Tip>

## Bulk resolving follow-ups

If multiple follow-ups share the same resolution — for example, a class was away on a school trip — you can resolve them all at once. Select the relevant rows using the checkboxes, then choose **Resolve Selected** from the bulk actions menu. You'll be prompted to enter a single set of resolution notes that applies to all selected records.

## Finding open follow-ups

To review all unresolved follow-ups across your school, go to **Registers > Follow-Ups** in the navigation. The list defaults to showing **Pending** status. You can also filter by **Attendance Status** (Absent, Late, or Early Leave) to focus on a specific category.

The **Follow-Ups** tab on each register displays a badge with the count of pending follow-ups, so you can see at a glance which registers still need attention.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What gets recorded in the audit trail?">
    Every action on a follow-up — creation, updates, and resolution — is logged by Elimu Bora's activity audit system. The log records the action taken, who performed it, and when. This trail is read-only and cannot be edited, giving school leadership a reliable record for any disputes or safeguarding reviews.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can a resolved follow-up be reopened?">
    Once a follow-up is marked as **Resolved**, it cannot be moved back to Pending through the standard interface. If a resolution needs to be revisited, contact your system administrator.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who receives the follow-up assignment notification?">
    When a follow-up is created and assigned to a staff member or teacher, that person receives both an in-app notification and an email notification. The notification lets them know a follow-up requires their attention.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
