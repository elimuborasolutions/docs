A stock requisition is a formal internal request for items from the school's inventory. Any staff member — a teacher who needs exercise books, a librarian requesting reference materials, or catering staff needing kitchen supplies — can raise a requisition. The procurement team reviews the request, approves or rejects it, and fulfils it when the items are available. You receive a notification at every step.

## Who can raise a requisition

Any staff member with access to the Staff Panel can submit a stock requisition. You do not need special permissions to make a request — only the procurement team needs approval rights.

## Requisition lifecycle

Every requisition moves through the following stages:

```
Pending → Approved → Ordered → Fulfilled
       └→ Rejected
```

| Status | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Pending** | Your request has been submitted and is waiting for procurement review |
| **Approved** | Procurement has approved the request |
| **Ordered** | The item has been ordered from a supplier (if not currently in stock) |
| **Fulfilled** | The items have been issued to you — you can collect them |
| **Rejected** | The request was not approved; see the notes on the requisition for the reason |

## Creating a requisition

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Stock Requisitions">
    In the left navigation, go to **Inventory** > **Stock Requisitions**, then click **New requisition**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the product">
    Use the **Product** dropdown to search for and select the item you need. The catalogue shows all products managed by your school's procurement team.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the quantity">
    Enter the **Requested Quantity** — how many units you need.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a reason">
    Select the reason that best describes why you need the item:

    | Reason | When to use it |
    |---|---|
    | **Low Stock** | You are running low but have not completely run out |
    | **Out of Stock** | You have no more of the item left |
    | **New Requirement** | This is a new need that has not been requested before |
    | **Damaged Items** | Existing items are damaged and need to be replaced |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the priority">
    Choose the urgency of your request:

    | Priority | When to use it |
    |---|---|
    | **Low** | No immediate urgency; can wait for the next regular order |
    | **Medium** | Needed soon but not blocking any activity today |
    | **High** | Needed urgently; impacting teaching or operations |
    | **Urgent** | Critical — required immediately |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add notes (optional)">
    Use the **Notes** field to give the procurement team any additional context — for example, the specific class or activity the items are for.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit the requisition">
    Click **Save**. Your requisition is submitted with a status of **Pending** and the procurement team is notified immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
You will receive a notification by email and in-app each time the status of your requisition changes — when it is approved, when it moves to ordered, and when it is fulfilled and ready for collection. You do not need to follow up manually.
</Note>

## Approval workflow

Once you submit a requisition, it lands in the procurement team's queue.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Approving a requisition (procurement staff)">
    Open the requisition from **Inventory** > **Stock Requisitions** and review the details. If the request is valid and the item is available or can be ordered, change the status to **Approved**. The requester receives a notification.

    If the item needs to be sourced from a supplier, update the status to **Ordered** once the purchase order has been raised.

    When the items are ready to hand over, mark the requisition as **Fulfilled**. The requester receives a final notification that their items are ready.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Rejecting a requisition (procurement staff)">
    If the request cannot be fulfilled — for example, because the item is not in the catalogue or the request is outside budget — change the status to **Rejected**. Add a note explaining the reason so the requester understands the outcome.

    The requester is notified of the rejection automatically.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Warning>
Once a requisition is **Fulfilled** or **Rejected**, it cannot be re-opened. If a fulfilled requisition needs a follow-up (for example, the quantity was incorrect), raise a new requisition.
</Warning>

## Tracking your requisitions

To check on the status of your requests:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Stock Requisitions">
    Go to **Inventory** > **Stock Requisitions**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter the list">
    Use the filters at the top of the list to narrow down by **Status** or search by your own name to see only your requisitions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open a requisition for details">
    Click on any requisition to see its full details, including the current status, any notes added by the procurement team, and the history of status changes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
If your requisition has been sitting in **Pending** for longer than expected, you can follow up directly with the procurement team. The requisition record gives you the reference details to quote when asking for an update.
</Tip>
