Every student in Elimu Bora has a digital wallet with a running balance. Wallets are created automatically when a student is enrolled. The wallet is designed for day-to-day school spending — canteen purchases, supplies, activity fees, and any other service your school charges to students directly. Guardians fund the wallet in advance, and deductions are recorded as the school charges for services.

---

## Topping up a wallet

When a guardian deposits money for their child's wallet — either by paying at the school or transferring funds — you record a credit to increase the wallet balance.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Wallets">
    Navigate to **Finance** → **Wallets**. You will see all student wallets with their current balances.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the student's wallet">
    Search for the student by name or admission number and click their wallet to open it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add Credit">
    On the wallet detail page, click **Add Credit** to open the top-up form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the credit details">
    Fill in the amount to add (in KES), the transaction date, and an optional reference (such as a receipt number or M-Pesa transaction code).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the credit">
    Click **Save**. The wallet balance updates immediately and the transaction is logged. The guardian receives an automatic notification confirming the funds have been added.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If you need to top up wallets for many students at once — for example, at the start of term — use the **CSV bulk import** instead of adding credits one by one. Go to **Finance** → **Wallets** → **Import** and upload a spreadsheet with student admission numbers and credit amounts. See the [bulk imports guide](/school/bulk-imports) for the template and column format.
</Tip>

## Recording a deduction

A debit reduces a student's wallet balance when the school charges for a service, such as a canteen meal or a stationery purchase.

Deductions are typically recorded by the relevant department (catering staff for canteen purchases, for example) rather than by the finance office. Each debit is logged with the amount, timestamp, and a description so there is a full record of what the wallet was used for.

To record a manual debit:

1. Open the student's wallet from **Finance** → **Wallets**.
2. Click **Debit Wallet**.
3. Enter the amount, date, and a description of the charge.
4. Click **Save**.

The guardian receives an automatic notification when a debit is recorded.

---

## Viewing transaction history

To see a full record of all credits and debits on a student's wallet:

1. Go to **Finance** → **Wallets** and open the student's wallet.
2. Click the **Transaction History** tab.

Each row shows the transaction date, type (Credit or Debit), amount, balance before the transaction, balance after, transaction ID, and a description. You can filter by date range to view a specific period.

---

## Low balance alerts

You can configure a minimum balance threshold for your school. When a student's wallet falls below that threshold, the guardian receives an automatic notification so they can top up before the balance runs out. This prevents situations where a student cannot access the canteen or a service because their wallet is empty.

The alert fires automatically — you do not need to check balances manually.

---

## Guardian notifications summary

Guardians are notified automatically for the following wallet events:

| Event | Notification |
|---|---|
| Credit added to wallet | Wallet funded notification via email and in-app |
| Debit recorded | Wallet debited notification via email and in-app |
| Balance falls below threshold | Low balance alert via email and in-app |
